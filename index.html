03 · Stakeholders

BrightHire speaks to four stakeholders in the interview interaction.

Each comes to the interview with a different goal — and a different reason it's hard. BrightHire's messaging primarily speaks to TA (the user) and People & HR Leaders (the buyer), while serving hiring managers and candidates through the experience the platform creates.

Primary · User
TA
Talent Acquisition
Who they are
Tip of the spear sourcing and screening talent.
What they want
Fill the top-of-funnel with the most qualified candidates.
Why it's hard
Applicant volume beyond bandwidth, AI making it harder to separate qualifications from buzzwords, and interviewing + note-taking + corralling feedback all at once — quality gives.
Served through the platform

Hiring Managers

Leaders for a specific function in their org.

Fill positions on their teams.

A real shot at proving fit, clear feedback if they don't, a human experience — and no ghosting.

High application volume makes landing interviews tough, ATS screens out qualified candidates, and overworked TA leads to inconsistent comms, ghosting, and no feedback.
SECTION II

The Moment

04 · The Moment

Hiring is in an AI doom loop.

Talent acquisition is one of the functions most disrupted by AI — and uniquely, the disruption is happening on both sides of the table. Candidates use AI to generate applications at scale; recruiters use AI to screen at scale. Neither side trusts what the other is sending — and the result is a hiring process where everyone is busier, fewer decisions are based on real signal, and trust keeps eroding.

300M

Volume crisis

Greenhouse processed 300 million applications in a single year. Recruiters are managing triple the volume per role — with no headcount growth.

Greenhouse, via From Day One (Aug 2025). Triple-volume figure per HR Dive (April 2026).
40-80%

AI on both sides

40–80% of candidates use AI to apply. 13% used generative AI in real-time during their interview.

DemandSage / Gartner 2025; Gartner Q3 2025.
8%

Trust has collapsed

Only 8% of job seekers think AI screening is fair. 26% trust AI to evaluate them. 53% were ghosted in 2026.

Greenhouse 2025 AI in Hiring Report; trust figure: Gartner July 2025; ghosting: Criteria Corp 2026 Candidate Experience Report.
The Takeaway
The answer to the doom loop isn't less AI — it's better AI applied to the right moment: the interview itself, where signal lives and trust can be rebuilt.
The phrase "AI doom loop" was coined by Greenhouse CEO Daniel Chait, Fortune, Nov 2025.

SECTION IV

The Narrative

06 · The Claim

The system of record for hiring decisions.

The way BrightHire wins in this crowded market is by claiming the one position no competitor can credibly own.

An ATS holds the candidate record. A notetaker holds the transcript. Only BrightHire holds the structured, auditable link between the conversation and the decision — which is the part that actually matters when leadership asks "why did we hire this person?"
01 · End-to-end

The system of record's shape.

Most competitors are point solutions — capture only, screening only, scheduling only. BrightHire spans the full workflow: Plan → Screen → Interview → Capture → Decide → Improve.

02 · Every side

Covers every side of the interview.

TA gets screening and capture. Hiring managers get structure and clarity. People leaders get defensible processes. Candidates get a real shot and real feedback. No competitor serves all four.

03 · Purpose-built

Purpose-built, with proof.

Six years of building AI for this specific function. 3M+ real interviews of training data — a corpus no generic notetaker, no ATS feature team, and no recently-pivoted competitor can match.

08 · The Zoom Question

On Zoom — distribution upside, positioning hazard.

The Zoom acquisition (December 2025) is the single most important strategic context for BrightHire right now. It needs deliberate messaging.

The Opportunity

Zoom hosts millions of interviews a year. The acquisition unlocks roughly 10–50x BrightHire's standalone TAM through Zoom's enterprise base.

The Risk

Zoom AI Companion is free with paid Zoom plans. The buyer's reflex question becomes: "Why pay extra for BrightHire when AI Companion is already bundled?"

The framing that resolves the tension:
Zoom AI Companion
  • Horizontal — for all meetings
  • Records and summarizes
  • For companies that want better notes
  • Free with Zoom
vs.
BrightHire
  • Vertical — purpose-built for hiring
  • Structures, scores, and decides
  • For companies that want better hires
  • A standalone platform that happens to run on Zoom (and Meet, and Teams)
Bottom line: AI Companion is horizontal notetaking applied to a vertical workflow. BrightHire is the vertical workflow. Different products. Different categories.
