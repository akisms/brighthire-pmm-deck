SECTION I

The Moment

03 · The Moment

Hiring is in an AI doom loop.

Talent acquisition is one of the functions most disrupted by AI — and uniquely, the disruption is happening on both sides of the table. Candidates use AI to generate applications at scale; recruiters use AI to screen at scale. Neither side trusts what the other is sending — and the result is a hiring process where everyone is busier, fewer decisions are based on real signal, and trust keeps eroding.

300M

Volume crisis

Greenhouse processed 300 million applications in a single year. Recruiters are managing triple the volume per role — with no headcount growth.

Greenhouse, via From Day One (Aug 2025). Triple-volume figure per HR Dive (April 2026).
40%+

The application is no longer a signal

At least 40% of candidates use AI to generate their applications — and that number is rising fast. The resume, the cover letter, even the first-round message all increasingly come from a model, not a person. The first place a recruiter can actually meet the candidate is the interview itself.

DemandSage 2025. Higher-end estimates from Gartner put the figure as high as 80%.
66%

Trust has collapsed — and it's costing pipeline

66% of U.S. adults say they would avoid applying for jobs that use AI in hiring decisions. Companies deploying AI badly aren't just creating bad candidate experiences — they're losing qualified pipeline before applications even arrive.

DemandSage 2025. Supporting: only 8% of job seekers think AI screening is fair (Greenhouse 2025 AI in Hiring Report).
The Takeaway
The answer to the doom loop isn't less AI — it's better AI applied to the right moment: the interview itself, where signal lives and trust can be rebuilt.
The phrase "AI doom loop" was coined by Greenhouse CEO Daniel Chait, Fortune, Nov 2025.

08 · The Zoom Question

On Zoom — distribution upside, positioning hazard.

The Zoom acquisition (December 2025) is the single most important strategic context for BrightHire right now. It needs deliberate messaging.

The Opportunity

Zoom hosts millions of interviews a year. The acquisition unlocks roughly 10–50x BrightHire's standalone TAM through Zoom's enterprise base.

The Risk

Zoom AI Companion is free with paid Zoom plans. The buyer's reflex question becomes: "Why pay extra for BrightHire when AI Companion is already bundled?"

The framing that resolves the tension:
Zoom AI Companion
  • Horizontal — for all meetings
  • Records and summarizes
  • For companies that want better notes
  • Free with Zoom
vs.
BrightHire
  • Vertical — purpose-built for hiring
  • Structures, scores, and decides
  • For companies that want better hires
  • A standalone platform that happens to run on Zoom (and Meet, and Teams)
Bottom line: AI Companion is horizontal notetaking applied to a vertical workflow. BrightHire is the vertical workflow. Different products. Different categories.
